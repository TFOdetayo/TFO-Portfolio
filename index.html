Everybody Has a Story

Talents are not beautiful until they are discovered and put to work. I realized this in 2021 when I developed an interest in Web design. After researching and speaking to senior colleagues, I channeled my passion into reality and began writing codes to create visually appealing, user-friendly, and innovative web pages.

Being an Obafemi Awolowo University student made this journey even easier. I had access to mentors and friends doing the same thing I was passionate about. It all started with YouTube videos, Jennifer O’Reilly’s Web development textbooks, online courses, personal classes with mentors, and continuous practice.

The following year, I enrolled in an ALX Software Engineering course on scholarship, which broadened my scope of website development with training in technical skills, teamwork, version control, C programming language, and Python programming language. However, I could not continue after school resumed, and classes were physically and mentally demanding.

Later, I enrolled in the Meta Specialization for Frontend Web Designers and enjoyed the nine courses. While I have had to pause, resume, learn, relearn, and unlearn for various reasons, I have come to understand that the most celebrated solutions in the world were once hidden beneath problems, awaiting someone to unravel them.

Over the years, I have focused more on my web development career. I have worked on several projects, including personal projects and collaborations with other developers. I am currently looking for opportunities to work with companies and individuals who share my passion for web development.
